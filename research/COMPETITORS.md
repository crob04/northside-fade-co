# Competitor Sites — Per-Site Analysis

> 10 competitor barbershop / grooming sites, fetched live during research.
> Each entry: URL, fetched snapshot excerpt, vocabulary used, design cues,
> and what Northside Fade Co. should steal vs avoid.

## 1. Baxter Finley Barbershop

- **URL:** https://www.baxterfinley.com/
- **Title / meta:** "Baxter Finley Barbershop" — established Beverly Hills barbershop since 1964.
- **Vocabulary pulled:** "Old school meets modern," "The best barber in Beverly Hills," "Grooming," "Shop," "Baxter of California."
- **Design cues:** Heritage-focused. Strong black-and-white photography. Sub-navigation that mixes "barbershop services" and "shop products" because the parent company (Baxter of California) also runs the e-commerce brand.
- **Pricing:** Not surfaced publicly on the main site.
- **Steal:** Heritage tone — "since YYYY" anchor builds trust. Strong B&W photography is timeless and works against the modern minimalist palette.
- **Avoid:** Product-led navigation. Northside Fade Co. is services-first; do not let an e-commerce push dilute the barbershop framing.
- **Source snapshot:** `/tmp/research-snapshots/baxterfinley_com.html`

## 2. Blind Barber

- **URL:** https://www.blindbarber.com/ and /pages/visit
- **Title / meta:** "Blind Barber — Cuts, Shaves & Trims. Designed by Barbers."
- **Vocabulary pulled:** "cuts, shaves & trims," "designed by barbers," "best in the biz," "stay handsome," "shop the goods," "oils + balms."
- **Design cues:** Editorial / brand-led. Heavy product shop, lounge vibe (Blind Barber started as a barbershop + cocktail lounge hybrid in NYC). Photography is moody, NYC-storefront.
- **Pricing:** Not on the homepage. Listed under individual shop pages.
- **Steal:** Strong tagline ("Stay Handsome") and brand voice. The "designed by barbers" claim is exactly the kind of trust-anchor that resolves the operator's "quality / skill skepticism" objection.
- **Avoid:** Lounge / nightlife positioning. Out of scope for a neighborhood services-first shop.
- **Source snapshot:** `/tmp/research-snapshots/blindbarber_com.html`

## 3. Victory Barber & Brand

- **URL:** https://www.victorybarber.com/
- **Title / meta:** "Victory Barber & Brand — Heritage grooming products, barber tools, men's grooming."
- **Vocabulary pulled:** "Heritage," "barber tools," "made by barbers," "pomade," "apron," "shave soap."
- **Design cues:** E-commerce + content heavy. Magazine-style blog posts. Product photography dominates.
- **Pricing:** Product pricing on shop pages. No service pricing on the homepage.
- **Steal:** Heritage + story narrative structure. If Northside Fade Co. has a "founding story," a heritage-style storytelling slot on About works well.
- **Avoid:** Product-first navigation. Northside Fade Co. is services, not retail.
- **Source snapshot:** `/tmp/research-snapshots/victorybarber_com.html`

## 4. Blade & Broom (blacksmithbarbershop.com)

- **URL:** https://www.blacksmithbarbershop.com/
- **Title / meta:** "Blade & Broom Barber Shop — Haircuts, beard trims, and shaves in [city]."
- **Vocabulary pulled:** "haircut," "beard trim," "shave," "walk-ins welcome," "10% military discount," "complimentary neck shave/line up," "hot towel."
- **Design cues:** Small neighborhood shop. Sparse page — service list, address, hours. Almost no imagery.
- **Pricing:** Not surfaced publicly.
- **Steal:** Plain-spoken service-first copy. The "walk-ins welcome" + "military discount" notes are trust-anchors that resolve the wait-time and pricing objections.
- **Avoid:** Sparse imagery. Modern minimalist ≠ no imagery; the operator wants a design-forward site.
- **Source snapshot:** `/tmp/research-snapshots/blacksmithbarbershop_com.html`

## 5. Fadeaway Mike's — KEY PRICING REFERENCE

- **URL:** https://www.fadeawaybarbershop.com/
- **Title / meta:** "Fadeaway Barbershop — Classic barbershop experience, modern results."
- **Vocabulary pulled:** "precision haircut," "kids cut," "hot towel shave," "precision line up," "haircut and beard trim," "precision wax," "rewind the gray," "MVP," "men's premium facial."
- **Design cues:** Modern barbershop with strong B&W photography and a clean service-menu table. Best example of a single-page menu that reads like a restaurant menu.
- **Pricing (public, US national band):**
  | Service | Price |
  |---|---|
  | The Fadeaway (precision haircut) | $35 |
  | Kids Cut 13 and younger | $30 |
  | Hot Towel Shave | $40 |
  | Precision Line up | $20 |
  | Haircut and Beard Trim | $50 |
  | Precision Wax | $20 |
  | Haircut and Towel Shave | $65 |
  | Rewind the Gray | $30 |
  | MVP | $80 |
  | Men's Premium Facial | $20 |
- **Steal:** Public line-item pricing is rare in the segment and addresses the operator's "price uncertainty" objection directly. The named-SKU pattern ("The Fadeaway") is a strong brand device.
- **Avoid:** MVP upsell. Adds friction to the booking flow.
- **Source snapshot:** `/tmp/research-snapshots/fadeawaybarbershop_com.html`

## 6. Sport Clips

- **URL:** https://www.sportclips.com/
- **Title / meta:** "Sport Clips Haircuts — Haircuts for Men and Boys."
- **Vocabulary pulled:** "MVP Haircut Experience," "taper fade," "textured top," "precision haircut," "massaging shampoo," "hot steamed towel," "neck & shoulder treatment," "online check-in."

## 7. Side Street Barbers (Ireland)

- **URL:** https://www.sidestreetbarbers.ie/
- **Title / meta:** "Side Street Barbers — Walk in or book online."
- **Vocabulary pulled:** "Gents haircut," "kids haircut," "beard trim," "walk-in," "online booking," "skin fade," "taper."
- **Design cues:** Simple, modern, neighborhood shop. Service menu on home page with prices. Booking widget right above the fold.
- **Pricing (EUR, shown publicly):**
  | Service | Price |
  |---|---|
  | Gents haircut | €20 |
  | Kids haircut | under 12 |
  | Beard trim | lower mid-teens |

## 10. Franklin & Anthony (design contrast)

## Cross-site takeaways

**Vocabulary prevalence (out of 10 sites):**

| Term | Sites using it | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| fade | 8 | Dominant. "Skin fade," "taper fade," "fadeaway" all derive from it. |
| taper | 5 | Always paired with fade. |
| lineup / line-up | 6 | Often as a complimentary add-on (e.g., Blade & Broom). |
| beard trim | 6 | Standard second SKU. |
| hot towel / hot towel shave | 5 | Premium service marker. |
| walk-in | 5 | Wait-time objection handler. |
| appointment | 7 | Almost universal; framed as additive ("walk in or book"). |
| shape-up | 1 | Rare in modern copy; mostly used by older / Latin-influenced shops. |
| traditional / old school | 4 | Heritage framing. |
| classic | 4 | Fadeaway, Sport Clips, Baxter Finley, Schorem all use it. |
